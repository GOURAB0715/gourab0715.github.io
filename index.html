01 About Me

I am a PhD scholar in Computer Science at Shiv Nadar Institution of Eminence (Delhi-NCR), working at the intersection of algorithm design and theoretical computer science. My research investigates the mathematical structures underlying efficient computation.

I hail from Kabilpur, a small village in the North 24 Parganas district of West Bengal. My academic journey began at Ramakrishna Mission institutions, where a disciplined and rigorous environment shaped my scholarly outlook and commitment to intellectual honesty.

I completed my B.Sc. in Computer Science from Ramakrishna Mission Vivekananda Centenary College, Rahara, and my M.Sc. from Ramakrishna Mission Residential College, Narendrapur. My current doctoral research focuses on algorithm design and analysis, with an interest in problems at the boundary of theory and efficient computation.

04 Projects
Randomness Testing Exploiting Prime Density
Supervisor : Dr. Arpita Maitra, Associate Professor, IAI - TCG CREST

Analyzed binary strings to assess statistical randomness using NIST Statistical Tests and Die-Harder tests. Developed a novel statistical test based on the Coupon Collector Problem and Kullback–Leibler Divergence to classify binary sequences as random or non-random, exploiting structural properties tied to prime number density.

Randomness Testing KL-Divergence Coupon Collector NIST Tests Statistical Analysis

Developed a full-stack web application enabling user authentication via application IDs and passwords. New users can register by providing personal details to generate a unique application ID. The system demonstrates end-to-end form validation, session management, and database-backed user records.
